Congratulations on being part of this elite group of Data Scientists within Accenture

Master Data Science Challenges represent a series of Data Science problems that participants would try to solution. Each challenge represents a typical client Data Science problem. Paricipants compete as teams to build the "Best Solution" based on a training dateset.

The solution modeling efficacy is determined based on performance on test dataset(s). The metric used would be pre determined for each challenge. Additionally, each team is required to submit an Executive Summary(maximum of two pages) explaining the solution. Each participant would accumulate Challenge points based upon the above submissions

No Hardware/ common computation environment will be provided for the competition

RULES AT A GLANCE:

Participate as teams. Any team member can submit and submission will reflect on the team’s score.

Teams can submit multiple entries for modelling results. The "best" score would be used to allocate challenge points. A daily and overall cap may be introduced if gaming is suspected.

Use of any External data is not permitted.

Any modelling technique can be leveraged.

ONLY the last Executive Summary submission before the deadline would be evaluated. Subjective evaluation done by SME’s are final

MathLogic reserves the right to modify rules in order to achieve the outcome desired by Accenture.


How can I earn Challenge Points?

Each challenge provides an opportunity to earn a maximum of 100 challenge points. Challenge points are allocated for modeling efficacy as well as executive summary. All members of team earn the same amount of challenge points

MDSC01: Predicting Credit Card Fraud

Credit card fraud transactions is one of biggest issues faced by consumers and credit card issuers. Estimated world-wide loss due to fraud is USD 28.65 Billion* in 2019 to credit card issuers. A key part of fraud mitigation is fraud detection by leveraging sophisticated analytics in identifying fraudulent transaction on real time basis Challenge is to identify potential fraudulent credit card transaction. In MDSC01, you will build a binary classification model based on Train dataset and then use it to predict possibly fraudulent transactions on Test dataset.
